# Jordy Personas and Jobs-to-be-Done

## Persona 1: Beginner Investor (Primary)
- Profile: First 6-18 months in equities, limited finance vocabulary, low confidence.
- Goal: Understand whether a stock is worth further study without reading full filings.
- Risk preference: Conservative to balanced.

### Top Tasks
1. Ask Jordy to analyze one stock in plain language.
2. Compare 2-3 stocks in same sector.
3. Understand key risks before buying.
4. Save stocks to watchlist.
5. Track if thesis improves or worsens.

### Pain Points
- Cannot interpret P/E, debt, volatility quickly.
- Information overload from conflicting sources.
- Unclear next step after reading analysis.

### Success Criteria
- Gets a clear verdict + reasons in under 2 minutes.
- Understands top 3 risks and what to monitor.
- Can take one explicit next action (watchlist/compare/deeper analysis).

## Persona 2: Intermediate Investor
- Profile: 2-5 years investing, comfortable with ratios, compares opportunities actively.
- Goal: Rank opportunities faster and validate thesis quality.
- Risk preference: Balanced.

### Top Tasks
1. Run sector-wide idea scan for USA/India.
2. Sort by composite score and risk profile.
3. Inspect quant vs qual disagreement.
4. Review evidence behind each driver.
5. Build a short candidate list with constraints.

### Pain Points
- Manual aggregation across price, filings, and news is slow.
- Difficulty separating signal from sentiment noise.
- Hard to keep historical context of previous analysis.

### Success Criteria
- Produces shortlist in under 10 minutes.
- Can justify each pick using evidence panel.
- Can explain why rejected names were excluded.

## Persona 3: Advanced Retail/Power User
- Profile: Data-driven user, expects transparency and control.
- Goal: Audit model outputs and customize workflow.
- Risk preference: Balanced to aggressive.

### Top Tasks
1. Validate feature inputs used by scores.
2. Review scoring breakdown and confidence.
3. Compare horizons (short vs long term).
4. Monitor watchlist drift and risk-trigger changes.
5. Export or copy structured output.

### Pain Points
- Black-box recommendations reduce trust.
- Missing provenance and timestamps for evidence.
- Lack of configurable assumptions per strategy.

### Success Criteria
- Can trace each recommendation to inputs and evidence.
- Can detect stale data or low-confidence outputs quickly.
- Can reuse outputs in their own research workflow.
